Zoo Life As A Giraffe

Feeding

How A Zoo Tends To A Giraffe's Natural Diet

In the wild, giraffes primarily feed on a variety of leaves, fruits, and flowers found in the treetops of the African savanna. Their long necks allow them to reach high branches, where they can access a wide array of plant species that most other herbivores can't. However, in a zoo setting, giraffes’ diets are carefully curated by zookeepers to meet their nutritional needs. While they still enjoy their favorite foods—such as acacia leaves—zoo giraffes are often fed a specially balanced mix of hay, fresh greens, and nutrient-rich supplements to ensure they stay healthy and thrive in captivity.

Mimicking Nature

How A Zoo Recreates A Girrafe's Habitat

In a zoo setting, giraffes are provided with expansive enclosures that closely resemble their natural African savanna habitats. These spaces include plenty of room for them to roam and engage in natural behaviors such as browsing on trees, interacting with other giraffes, and even grazing on grasses. Zoo teams work hard to ensure that giraffes feel comfortable and are mentally stimulated. Enclosures are designed with tall trees, elevated platforms, and open spaces that mimic the wide, open landscapes giraffes naturally inhabit. Visitors can often see giraffes interacting with each other or calmly munching on their favorite leaves, giving a glimpse into their world in a safe and controlled environment.
